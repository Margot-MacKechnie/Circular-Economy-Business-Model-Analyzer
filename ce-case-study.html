Background

Context & Motivation

The circular economy is no longer a niche sustainability concept — it is a regulatory and strategic priority for businesses operating in the European Union. The EU's Circular Economy Action Plan (2020), the Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD), and the Right to Repair Directive (2024) are collectively creating real pressure on companies to understand and demonstrate circular business model practices.

Despite this, most available circular economy assessment tools are either inaccessible (locked behind enterprise software or consultancy engagements) or too surface-level to drive real strategic decision-making. I built this tool to fill that gap: a rigorous, framework-grounded assessment that any business could complete independently and act on immediately.

Why it matters for Nordic markets: Denmark, Finland, and Sweden have some of the world's most advanced national circular economy strategies. Danish companies in particular are actively integrating circular principles into core business model design — making this kind of analytical literacy a genuine differentiator for sustainability professionals working in the region.
The Problem

What Gap Does This Fill?

❌ The Problem

Existing CE assessment tools are either high-cost enterprise platforms, overly simplified checklists, or academic frameworks with no practical UX. Organizations lack a free, structured, actionable way to audit their circular business model and know where to focus.

✅ The Opportunity

A well-designed, self-serve assessment tool — grounded in globally recognized frameworks and producing a composite score, radar visualization, and prioritized recommendations — could make circular business model analysis accessible to any organization.

The tool needed to balance two competing demands: enough rigor to be credible with sustainability professionals and enough accessibility to be completable without specialist knowledge. It also needed to be fully client-side to ensure it could be deployed as a static GitHub Pages site with no backend infrastructure or maintenance overhead.

The Solution

A Five-Stage Assessment Framework

The tool guides users through five structured stages, each addressing a distinct dimension of circular business model analysis. The stages are designed to progressively build context, so that by the time a user reaches the results panel, the scoring and recommendations are grounded in a rich picture of their specific model.

Stage 1 — Company Profile captures sector, size, geography, and circularity maturity, and asks users to select relevant ReSOLVE strategies upfront. This frames the entire assessment and allows recommendations to be sector-contextual rather than generic.

Stage 2 — Circular Business Model Canvas adapts Osterwalder's original nine-block canvas for circular economy principles, with each block reinterpreted through a circular lens: take-back programs, circular revenue streams, reverse logistics partnerships, and outcome-based value propositions.

Stage 3 — Material Loop Mapping addresses the physical reality of circular economy: where does material go at end-of-life? Users map their loops, assess material risks and dependencies, and score their loop maturity across five sub-dimensions.

Stage 4 — ReSOLVE Scoring rates the business against all six Ellen MacArthur Foundation ReSOLVE dimensions — Regenerate, Share, Optimise, Loop, Virtualise, Exchange — with free-text evidence fields to encourage reflection rather than rote scoring.

Stage 5 — Results & Report synthesizes everything into a composite circularity score (0–100), a radar chart, a dimension-by-dimension breakdown, dynamic strategic recommendations ranked by priority, a 90-day quick wins checklist, and a one-click PDF export.

Key Features

What the Tool Does

📊

Composite Circularity Score

Weighted 40/30/30 across ReSOLVE, Canvas, and Material Loops — with a tiered classification from Linear Model to Leading Circular Model.

🕸️

ReSOLVE Radar Chart

Live Chart.js radar visualization showing performance across all six circular dimensions — a standard output in professional CE assessments.

💡

Dynamic Recommendations

8 possible recommendations, each triggered by specific score thresholds. Prioritized as High, Medium, or Quick Win based on the user's actual inputs — not static advice.

✅

90-Day Quick Wins

Score-driven checklist of immediate, low-barrier actions — generated from gap analysis across all 17 scored sub-dimensions.

📄

PDF Report Export

Client-side PDF generation via jsPDF, auto-populated with company profile, all dimension scores, and bar chart visualizations — no server required.

📖

Methodology Explainer

A companion page documenting all nine sections of the theoretical and scoring framework, with full academic citations — positioning the tool as research-grounded, not just a UI.

Design Decisions

Key Technical & Design Choices

DecisionChoiceRationale
ArchitectureStatic HTML/CSS/JSNo backend means no hosting cost, no maintenance, and instant GitHub Pages deployment — critical for a portfolio project that needs to stay live and accessible.
Scoring modelWeighted composite (40/30/30)ReSOLVE is weighted highest because it captures strategic intent — the most meaningful signal of genuine circular transformation — over operational metrics alone.
RecommendationsScore-threshold triggeredStatic recommendations ignore user-specific gaps. Threshold logic ensures each user sees the advice most relevant to their weakest dimensions, making the tool genuinely useful rather than decorative.
VisualizationRadar chart (Chart.js)Radar charts are the standard output format in professional CE assessments — including the Ellen MacArthur Foundation's Circulytics tool — so using one signals domain literacy.
PDF exportjsPDF (client-side)Server-side PDF generation would require a backend. jsPDF runs entirely in the browser, maintaining the static architecture while still delivering a professional report artifact.
UX flowTabbed multi-stageA single long form is cognitively overwhelming for a 17-dimension assessment. Tabbed stages create natural breakpoints, reduce dropout, and allow progressive disclosure of complexity.
Framework choiceReSOLVE + adapted BMCBoth are internationally recognized and used by practitioners, not just academics. Grounding the tool in established frameworks gives it credibility with the sustainability professionals it targets.

Reflections

What I'd Do Differently

  • 🔢 Sector-specific weighting. The current scoring model weights all sub-dimensions equally within each category. In practice, a fashion company's most critical circular gap is almost always material loops, while a software company's is virtualisation. A future version would apply sector-aware weighting to make recommendations more precise.
  • 📥 Data-backed scoring prompts. Each slider currently relies entirely on self-assessment. Prompting users with quantitative anchors — "score 8 if >50% recycled content, score 4 if 10–25%" — would reduce optimism bias and make scores more comparable across organizations.
  • 📈 Longitudinal tracking. The tool produces a point-in-time snapshot. A persistent storage layer would allow organizations to retake the assessment over time and visualize their circular maturity trajectory — far more valuable for ongoing strategy work.
  • 🌐 Multilingual support. Given the tool's explicit orientation toward Nordic and EU markets, Danish and Finnish translations would meaningfully expand its practical reach and demonstrate market-specific intent to prospective employers in those regions.
